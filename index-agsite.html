Invocation

Invocation gives a sense of gratitude for Patanjali, for coming up withthis system. The Invocation settles the mind and the body so that the attention can go towards the practice, its a buffer from the world outside to the practice

  • Silence

    "The silence is the source of all experience, the silence at the end of Om it is not just the absence of noise, it refers to the Consciousness. Step back from all experiences that appear and disappear, into the silence of the unchanging. Universe here is all that we experience in waking, dream, and deep sleep. Silence is the cessation of the universe.

  • Invocation to Patanjali

    Yogena chittasya pade na vacham malam sharirasya cha vaidyakena |
    Yopa karotam pravaram muninam Patamjalim pranjaliranatosmi ||
    Abahu purashakaram shanka chakrasidharinam |
    Sahasra shirasam svetam prana ma mi Patanjallim
    Hari: OM

  • Om Shanti Shanti Shanti

    First Shanti points to the the environment and all of the calamities that have the potential to unfold, the second Shanti points to all of the relationships in the world and the potential for disturbance in all of those, the third Shanti refers to the relationship with the mind, other aspects are fine and the mind is capable of causing the most havoc. We chant Shantish Shantish Shantihi, to calm all three states.
