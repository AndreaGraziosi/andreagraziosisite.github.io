  • Silence

    "The silence is the source of all experience, the silence at the end of Om it is not just the absence of noise, it refers to the Consciousness. Step back from all experiences that appear and disappear, into the silence of the unchanging. Universe here is all that we experience in waking, dream, and deep sleep. Silence is the cessation of the universe.

  • Invocation

    Incorporate an attitude of gratitude in beginning of your practice, you are the sacred being intended to receive the blessings of yoga. The invocation gives a sense of gratitude for Patanjali for coming up with the system of yoga. Chanting this, settles the mind and the body so that the attention can go towards the practice, its a buffer from all that is happening in world and this activity that you are about to start.

  • Om Shanti Shanti Shanti

    The first Shanti points the calamities that have the potential to unfold, the second Shanti points to all of the relationships in the world and the potential for disturbance, the third Shanti refers to the relationship with the mind, for it is capable of causing the most havoc. May we only experience Shanti despite those three types of relating to the environment around us.
